Abstract

This paper introduces the "Cosmic Pressure and Containment (CPC)" theory, based on the idea that the universe maintains its structure through a balance between internal pressure and external containment. It challenges the gravity-only model and offers new explanations for cosmic expansion, galactic motions, and light behavior.

1. Introduction

Modern cosmology explains universal structure and motion primarily through gravity, under General Relativity. However, observations such as accelerated expansion, galaxy rotation curves, and stability of cosmic structures call for alternative models. CPC suggests that cosmic pressure, balanced by external containment, governs universal stability and introduces a new paradigm.

  1. Ongoing Expansion: Galaxies are receding, suggesting an internal pressure counteracting gravity.
  2. Stable Cosmic Balance: Gravity alone would cause collapse, but cosmic balance implies containment.
  3. Black Holes & Neutron Stars: Their containment despite immense gravity indicates pressure boundaries.
  4. CMB Uniformity: The cosmic microwave background‚Äôs uniformity suggests evenly distributed pressure.
  5. Matter-Antimatter Asymmetry: May arise due to containment's role in post-Big Bang evolution.
  6. Quantum Vacuum Pressure: Casimir Effect shows pressure exists even in vacuum.
  7. Limitations of Gravity Theories: Failure to unify with quantum mechanics supports pressure-based modeling.
  8. Cosmic Boundaries: Suggest containment, since infinite energy would be required otherwise.
  9. Architectural Analogy: Universe as a bubble or dome balanced by internal pressure and external shell.
  10. Biological/Natural Pressure Systems: From brain to oceans, pressure governs stable systems, possibly the universe too.

8. Flaws in Mainstream Models

  • General Relativity: Fails to explain expansion without dark energy.
  • Newtonian Gravity: Inapplicable at cosmic scale and neglects pressure.
  • Dark Energy Models: Based on invisible assumptions.
  • String Theory: Overly complex and lacks empirical proof.

9. Strengths of CPC

  • Explains expansion without dark energy.
  • Free of singularities.
  • Parallels with real-world pressure systems.

10. Conclusion

The CPC theory provides a pressure-based model for understanding cosmic structure and movement, avoiding shortcomings of gravity-centric models.
