Abstract

This theory proposes that the universe maintains its structure through a balance of internal cosmic pressure and an external containment force. It explains cosmic expansion, photon behavior, and structural stability without invoking dark energy or singularities.

1. Introduction

Modern cosmology heavily relies on gravity. However, observations like accelerating expansion and large-scale structure demand alternative explanations. CPC theory posits that internal pressure and external containment govern cosmic behavior.

2. The Cosmic Rupture

Just like a balloon bursts when internal pressure exceeds containment, the universe could face a rupture. Such an event would destabilize galaxies, stars, and even atomic structure.

3. Oceans: First Responders

Due to water's incompressibility, Earth's oceans would react first in a rupture scenario, possibly leading to global catastrophe.

4. Orbital Suspension

Instead of gravity alone, cosmic pressure maintains orbital positions of celestial bodies by balancing containment and pressure forces.

5. Light & Pressure

Photon behavior — redshift, bending, speed — is influenced by pressure gradients, not only gravity.

6. Theoretical Equations

  • Pressure-Containment Balance: Pi × A = Fc × A → Pi = Fc
  • Orbital Zones: Pr(r) = P₀ / (1 + kr²), where dPr/dr ≈ 0 → stable orbits
  • Redshift: Δλ ∝ ∇P · d

7. Critique of Other Theories

  • General Relativity predicts singularities and needs dark energy
  • Newtonian gravity fails at cosmic scales
  • Dark energy models are speculative
  • String theory lacks empirical grounding

8. Conclusion

CPC Theory provides a new paradigm for understanding the universe through dynamic pressure and structural containment — without resorting to invisible forces.
