Author: Usman Madni | Email: madniusman13@gmail.com

Abstract

This paper presents the Cosmic Pressure and Containment (CPC) theory, proposing that the structure and stability of the universe result from a balance between internal pressure and external containment, offering a new perspective beyond gravity-only models.

1. Introduction

Traditional cosmology attributes cosmic behavior to gravitational forces. However, observations like accelerating expansion and galaxy stability suggest that pressure may play a vital role. The CPC theory introduces pressure-containment equilibrium as a governing mechanism.

2. Ten Scientific Arguments for CPC Theory

  1. Universal Expansion: Contrary to gravitational expectations, galaxies move apart, suggesting an internal pressure.
  2. Maintained Balance: The universe has not collapsed, implying a counteracting force to gravity.

  4. CMBR Uniformity: Cosmic background radiation is almost identical everywhere ‚Äî indicating universal pressure.
  5. Matter-Antimatter Imbalance: May result from containment dynamics post-Big Bang.

  8. Universe Edges: A finite universe suggests structural boundaries or a containment field.
  9. Architectural Analogy: Like a pressurized bubble with surface tension maintaining form.
  10. Natural Pressure Systems: Like blood pressure or atmospheric systems ‚Äî nature favors pressure balance.

3. Cosmic Rupture Hypothesis

If internal pressure surpasses containment strength, a cosmic rupture may occur, disintegrating galaxies and matter.

4. Ocean Impact

Earth's oceans, being incompressible, would react first, absorbing shockwaves with devastating effect.

5. Why Objects Stay Suspended

Rather than gravity alone, CPC explains that objects float due to balanced cosmic pressure around them.

6. Light and Pressure
